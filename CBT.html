What is Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)?

CBT is a type of psychotherapy that helps you recognize and change negative thought patterns and behaviors. It is based on the idea that our thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are interconnected. By understanding and altering unhelpful thought patterns, you can improve emotional well-being and adopt healthier behaviors.

CBT Tools to Improve Mental Health

Here are some practical CBT tools you can use to start changing negative patterns and building positive thinking habits:

  • Cognitive Restructuring

    This tool helps you identify and challenge negative, irrational thoughts, replacing them with more balanced and realistic ones. For example, if you think, "I'm a failure," you can change that to "I made a mistake, but I can learn and improve." Itâ€™s a way to break free from the patterns of negative thinking.

  • Thought Log

    A thought log helps you track your thoughts, particularly the negative ones, and evaluate their accuracy. By writing them down and examining the evidence for and against them, you can gain insight into your thinking patterns and challenge unhelpful ones.

  • Behavioral Activation

    This tool helps you break the cycle of inactivity and depression. It involves identifying enjoyable or meaningful activities and planning them into your day. By re-engaging in these activities, you can improve mood and build positive momentum.
